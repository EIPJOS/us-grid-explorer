California power grid

California combines a large natural-gas fleet with substantial solar, hydro, storage, wind, and geothermal capacity. The result is a diverse system where resource mix and flexibility matter as much as the total capacity number.

What the data says

  • Oil & gas is the largest source at 40.2% of operating capacity.
  • The state reports 317 proposed generators totaling 21,042 MW.
  • The five largest mapped plants represent 9% of statewide operating capacity.
  • Data-center coverage includes 72 community-mapped locations and should not be treated as a complete inventory.
