Oregon power grid

Oregon's capacity mix is led by hydro, followed by natural gas and wind. Proposed capacity is large relative to the current system, making the state useful for studying a changing Western resource portfolio.

What the data says

  • Hydro is the largest source at 45.3% of operating capacity.
  • The state reports 32 proposed generators totaling 8,731 MW.
  • The five largest mapped plants represent 37% of statewide operating capacity.
  • Data-center coverage includes 111 community-mapped locations and should not be treated as a complete inventory.
