Energy storage

A system that takes in energy, retains it, and later returns electricity or another useful form of energy.

In plain languageA system that takes in energy, retains it, and later returns electricity or another useful form of energy.
Category
Technology
Primary reference
U.S. Energy Information Administration

Why it matters

How this term helps you read grid data

Storage can shift electricity across time, provide fast grid services, and reduce curtailment. Power capacity in MW does not reveal storage duration in hours.

Example

Energy storage in practice

A 100 MW battery with 400 MWh of energy capacity can discharge at maximum power for roughly four hours.
